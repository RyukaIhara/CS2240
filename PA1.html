How the Spaniards Colonized the Philippines

Ferdinand Magellan, a Portuguese navigator and explorer, led the first Spanish expedition to the Philippines when he arrived on Cebu in March 1521; he died tragically soon after on the nearby island of Mactan. After sending out three more expeditions that failed, King Philip II (for whom the islands are named) sent out Miguel LÃ³pez de Legazpi, who established the first permanent Spanish settlement in Cebu in 1565.


The Spanish metropolis of Manila was created in 1571, and by the end of the 16th century, they had conquered much of the coastal and lowland lands from Luzon to northern Mindanao. Friars marched with soldiers and quickly converted all of the soldiers to Roman Catholicism.
